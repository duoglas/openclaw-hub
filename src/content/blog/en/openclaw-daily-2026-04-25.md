## AI & Tech Daily | 2026-04-25 (Asia/Shanghai)

### Top 5 Signals

1. **Meta expands AWS partnership to run agentic AI on Graviton at large scale**
- **What happened:** AWS disclosed Meta signed up for large-scale Graviton deployment (starting in the tens of millions of cores) for code generation, real-time inference, search, and multi-step orchestration workloads.
- **Why it matters:** AI infrastructure competition is no longer GPU-only. CPU architecture, custom silicon, and system design are now part of frontline AI economics.
- **Likely impact:** Enterprise buying criteria will expand from model quality to total inference cost, energy efficiency, and operability.

2. **NVIDIA + Adobe + WPP push governable marketing agents into enterprise workflows**
- **What happened:** NVIDIA highlighted deeper collaboration with Adobe and WPP, combining Adobe CX/creative stack, WPP marketing workflows, and NVIDIA Nemotron + Agent Toolkit + OpenShell runtime.
- **Why it matters:** This is a shift from “content generation tools” to end-to-end autonomous execution pipelines that still require governance.
- **Likely impact:** Teams will accelerate agentic marketing adoption while increasing requirements on permission control, auditability, and brand safety.

3. **NVIDIA says GPT-5.5 now powers Codex and is deployed internally at scale**
- **What happened:** NVIDIA’s official post states Codex runs on GPT-5.5 over GB200 NVL72 systems, with broad internal usage.
- **Why it matters:** AI coding is moving from demo mode to production operations where throughput, policy controls, and governance matter as much as generation quality.
- **Likely impact:** Engineering orgs will split into two layers: assistant UX in front, sandboxed multi-agent execution environments behind.

4. **DeepSeek preview model reportedly optimized for Huawei chips** *(To Verify)*
- **What happened:** Reuters search snippets indicate a new preview model tuned for Huawei chips.
- **Why it matters:** If confirmed, this strengthens the “model + domestic chip” ecosystem integration signal.
- **Likely impact:** Greater demand for localization-ready deployment stacks and chip-adaptation benchmarks.
- **Verification note:** Current round only has L2 snippet evidence; no direct L1 source read captured yet.

5. **OpenAI reportedly accelerates Codex enterprise rollout via global consulting partners** *(To Verify)*
- **What happened:** Reuters search results suggest expanded partnership activity around enterprise Codex adoption.
- **Why it matters:** Competition is shifting from model capability alone to integration, delivery, and organizational transformation.
- **Likely impact:** More budget share may move to consultancies, cloud integrators, and implementation partners.
- **Verification note:** Currently retained as “To Verify” due to no direct-source fetch in this round.
